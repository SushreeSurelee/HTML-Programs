Ecotourism Resorts

Conservation is a big concern for many people nowadays. Ecotourism resorts allow people to have a fun vacation and feel like they’re leaving the world a little better off than it was. Sometimes these resorts are set up with a green design like solar panels on the roofs and rainwater cisterns.

All-Inclusive Resorts

All-inclusive resorts pride themselves on offering a huge selection of amenities. Most of these resorts operate under a fixed price model. Vacationers pay a lump fee per day and can enjoy most of what the resort has to offer.

Family-Focused Resorts

Some resorts work hard to cater to the children of the family. They may have extensive kids’ menus that work around the preferences of picky eaters, enrichment activities like nature tours, and plenty of games and arts or crafts opportunities.

Historic Resorts

Historic resorts focus on a historic site or archaeological area. These can range from converted Southern plantations to resorts near ancient Aztec temples.

Beach Resorts

Beach resorts are resorts that are located on the beach, or near the beach. They can differ from other types of resorts in terms of the activities and amenities that are available. In fact, beach resorts typically have a wide range of them, including swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.
